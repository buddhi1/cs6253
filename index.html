Heterogeneous Parallel and Distributed Computing for Efficient Geometric Computations over Big Spatiotemporal Datasets: A Survey

Space and time are two significant aspects of data objects from domains such as geographic information science, social science, neuroscience, epidemiology, meteorology, transportation, and criminology [1]. These data are collected using a variety of sensors and devices such as weather-capturing remote sensors, ground and ocean sensors, traffic cameras and sensors, medical imaging devices, handheld devices and social media, and simulation data. Data collections for past decades have large volumes of spatiotemporal data Currently, large volumes of such spatiotemporal data are available in different domains. These data require models, analyses, and mining tools to be used to understand and provide meaningful information. Spatial and time aspects of this data make relational databases and approaches based on them inefficient and in some cases incompatible. To manage spatiotemporal data and its properties, data structures and algorithms have been introduced in the past decades to mitigate the challenges and perform the computations efficiently. In this work, we focus on operations involved in geographic information systems and discuss state-of-the-art algorithms and data structures used to efficiently perform different computations.
